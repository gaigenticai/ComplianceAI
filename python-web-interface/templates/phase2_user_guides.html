KYC Review & Risk Scoring Guide

This comprehensive guide covers Phase 2: KYC review, identity verification, sanctions screening, and risk scoring in the ComplianceAI system.

1. Overview

Phase 2 focuses on comprehensive Know Your Customer (KYC) review processes, including identity verification, sanctions screening, Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) checks, and automated risk scoring. This phase integrates with external watchlist services and government databases to ensure regulatory compliance.

2. Key Features

  • Identity Verification: Multi-source validation using government databases and third-party services
  • Sanctions Screening: Real-time OFAC, EU, and UN sanctions list checking
  • PEP Screening: Politically Exposed Persons database matching
  • Risk Scoring: ML-based risk assessment and scoring algorithms
  • Adverse Media Monitoring: Negative news and reputation analysis
  • Automated Escalation: Rule-based case escalation for manual review

3. Identity Verification Process

Verification Methods

  • Document Verification: Cross-referencing extracted data with government databases
  • Biometric Matching: Facial recognition and fingerprint verification
  • Address Validation: Postal address verification services
  • Database Matching: Credit bureau and utility provider verification

Verification Confidence Levels

  • High Confidence: Multiple sources confirm identity (90-100% match)
  • Medium Confidence: Partial verification from reliable sources (70-89% match)
  • Low Confidence: Limited verification or conflicting information (0-69% match)
  • Failed Verification: Identity cannot be confirmed or appears fraudulent

4. Sanctions & PEP Screening

Sanctions Lists

  • OFAC SDN List: Office of Foreign Assets Control Specially Designated Nationals
  • EU Sanctions: European Union restrictive measures and asset freezes
  • UN Sanctions: United Nations Security Council sanctions
  • Local Lists: Country-specific sanctions and watchlists

PEP Categories

  • National Level: Heads of state, government ministers, parliament members
  • International Level: UN officials, international organization leaders
  • Regional Level: Provincial governors, regional officials
  • Close Associates: Family members and close business associates

Screening Process

  1. Name Matching: Fuzzy logic name matching against watchlists
  2. Alias Resolution: Alternative name and transliteration checking
  3. Date of Birth Matching: DOB verification within tolerance ranges
  4. Address Screening: Location-based sanctions checking
  5. Relationship Mapping: Network analysis for indirect connections

5. Risk Scoring Engine

Risk Factors

  • Customer Profile: Industry, geography, transaction patterns
  • Product/Service Risk: High-risk products and jurisdictions
  • Transaction Risk: Unusual patterns, high-value transfers
  • Channel Risk: Online vs. branch-based customer acquisition
  • Behavioral Risk: Account activity patterns and anomalies

Risk Scoring Algorithm

Risk Score = (Profile Risk × 0.3) + (Product Risk × 0.25) +
             (Transaction Risk × 0.25) + (Channel Risk × 0.15) +
             (Behavioral Risk × 0.05)

Risk Thresholds

  • Low Risk (0-30): Standard due diligence procedures
  • Medium Risk (31-70): Enhanced due diligence required
  • High Risk (71-100): Intensive due diligence and potential rejection

6. Automated Decision Engine

Decision Types

  • Auto-Approve: Low-risk cases meeting all criteria
  • Auto-Reject: Cases with critical compliance violations
  • Manual Review: Medium/high-risk cases requiring human assessment
  • Escalation: Cases requiring senior management approval

Decision Rules

  • Clear Pass: Risk score < 30 AND no sanctions/PEP matches
  • Clear Fail: Sanctions match OR risk score > 85
  • Review Required: Risk score 31-70 OR PEP match
  • Escalation: Risk score 71-84 OR close PEP associate

11. Troubleshooting

Common Issues

High False Positive Rate
Adjust fuzzy matching thresholds and implement whitelist for common names. Review name matching algorithms.
Third-Party Service Outages
Implement circuit breaker patterns and fallback to local databases. Configure multiple service providers.
PEP Screening Delays
Cache frequently accessed PEP data and implement batch processing for bulk screenings.
Risk Score Inconsistencies
Regularly validate risk scoring models and update factor weights based on regulatory changes.
